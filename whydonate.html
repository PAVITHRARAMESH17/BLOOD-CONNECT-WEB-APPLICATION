Why Donate Blood?

Because every drop counts, and every donor is a hero.

Why is Blood Donation Important?

Blood donation is a simple act that can save multiple lives. Every year, millions of people need blood transfusions due to accidents, surgeries, cancer treatment, chronic illnesses, and childbirth. Since there is no substitute for human blood, your donation becomes a lifeline.

Understanding Blood Groups

There are 8 main blood groups. Compatibility matters when donating or receiving blood:

O- is the universal donor for red blood cells, and AB+ is the universal recipient. Blood group compatibility is vital during transfusions to avoid complications.

How Much Blood Can You Donate?

  • An average adult has about 4.5 to 5.5 liters of blood.
  • During donation, only around 350-450 ml of blood is collected.
  • The human body regenerates this volume within 24 to 48 hours.
  • You can safely donate every 3 months.

Benefits of Donating Blood

  • Helps save up to 3 lives in one donation.
  • Reduces harmful iron stores in your body.
  • Improves cardiovascular health and reduces the risk of heart attacks.
  • Stimulates new blood cell production.
  • Provides a free mini health check-up (BP, hemoglobin, etc.).
  • Increases emotional well-being through the act of helping others.

Precautions & Safety Measures

  • Ensure you are well-hydrated and have eaten before donating.
  • Minimum age to donate: 18 years. Minimum weight: 50 kg.
  • All equipment used is sterile and for one-time use only.
  • Rest for 10-15 minutes after donation and avoid heavy exercise for the day.
  • Consult your doctor if you have medical conditions before donating.
